千面晷 The Thousand-Faced Sundial

清华大学法学院本科生 许昕月同学

用时钟来表示犹太民族这些年来发展的历程与文化底蕴，时针与分针的转动也恰恰标志着整个民族的生生不息，坚韧持重。作为一个创意作业，我首先希望自己能够做出一个实用的小装置，其次是能够蕴含该文化的深刻力量。考虑到我有激光雕刻的技术经验，于是选择DIY犹太文化钟表。该钟表一共耗时约3天制成，中间因为等待材料邮寄、雕刻尺寸误差等有过返工，好在最后按时完成，根据老师的建议又将原木色的钟表进行了上色，用白胶黏上彩砂后的钟表更加具有民族文化风情，图案也更加明媚。

This clock symbolizes the long course of Jewish history and its rich cultural heritage. The constant movement of the hour and minute hands reflects the resilience and vitality of the Jewish people. As a creative project, my initial goal was to design a practical device while also embedding it with profound cultural meaning. Given my prior experience with laser engraving, I decided to create a DIY Jewish cultural clock. The entire production took about three days, including time lost to material delivery delays and engraving errors that required rework. Fortunately, the project was completed on schedule. Following my instructor's advice, I later added color to the original wooden clock. By applying white glue and colored sand, the clock took on a brighter and more vibrant appearance, bringing out the cultural essence of its motifs.

金焰流光 Golden Flame Radiance

北京大学信息科学技术学院本科生 邱显童同学

本作品采用中国非物质文化遗产——掐丝珐琅工艺，制作犹太文化中极具代表性的九烛台。其蕴含的"光明"与"希望"主题，与中华文化中追求美好、向往光明的精神高度契合。掐丝珐琅工艺独特的金属丝线轮廓和彩砂颗粒质感，不仅能精致地表现烛台的造型，其材质本身（金丝与矿物砂），也赋予了作品一种永恒感和神圣感，与主题的庄重性相得益彰。作品背景采用犹太文化的标志性蓝白色调。如同蓝天下的火焰，象征着光明不息。这件作品不仅是一件装饰品，更是一座文化交流的桥梁。无论来自何种文化背景，人类对光明、团结与奇迹的信仰是相通的。

This piece employs the Chinese intangible cultural heritage of cloisonné craftsmanship to create the highly symbolic Jewish Menorah. Its themes of "light" and "hope" resonate deeply with the Chinese cultural spirit of pursuing beauty and aspiring toward illumination. The distinctive metal wire outlines and granular texture of colored sand in cloisonné work not only exquisitely capture the form of the Hanukkiah but also, through the materials themselves (gold wires and mineral sands), imbue the artwork with a sense of timelessness and sanctity that complements the solemnity of its theme. The background adopts the iconic blue and white tones of Jewish culture. Like flames against a blue sky, it symbolizes the eternal nature of light. More than merely a decorative object, this work serves as a bridge for cultural exchange. Regardless of cultural background, humanity's faith in light, unity, and miracles is universally shared.

犹太传统婚戒 Traditional Jewish Wedding Ring

清华大学新雅书院计算机专业本科生姚宇辰同学

在最初的犹太婚礼传统中，其实并无规定必需使用戒指；婚姻的合法性更多依靠契约、见证与象征性的“支付”来形成。但到了中世纪，戒指逐渐取代货币，成为婚礼上可被公众清楚“看见”的证物。婚戒的可见性、材质与象征意义被认为具有法律效力，因此戒指必须尺寸足够大，让见证人能明确看到赠予的动作，确保婚姻合法成立。自 13–14 世纪起，在欧洲的犹太社区内开始出现一种独特的婚戒形态—— 顶部带有微型“建筑体” 的建筑式婚戒（Architectural Wedding Ring）。这些戒指顶部往往呈现小型亭阁、穹顶、塔楼或多角建筑，部分甚至内藏可摇动的小球，在戴上戒指时发出声响，使赠予的行为不仅“视觉化”也“听觉化”，增强仪式意义。这些建筑式戒指并非仅仅是装饰，其象征对象被认为是犹太新家庭的“房屋”、 社区的会堂（Synagogue）以及最重要的象征重建已毁的耶路撒冷圣殿（Temple）。基于这一历史，我重新思考：如果以现代耶路撒冷的建筑或审美为灵感，延续中世纪的建筑式婚戒传统，会诞生怎样的作品？于是，本系列七枚戒指应运而生。

The class offered not only the expansion of knowledge but also pleasant surprises of cultural experience, allowing me to truly appreciate how a civilization has preserved its resilience and creativity throughout a long history. Whether it is the symbolic meaning behind dietary practices or the narratives and memories embodied in festival customs, everything felt novel and deeply fascinating. This course is rich in content and broad in perspective and it has been an extremely rare learning experience, one that truly made me feel that the world is far bigger than I had imagined. I thoroughly enjoyed this course and highly recommend it!
